Rules Of Throwball

.A team sport is throwball.
.There are twelve players on each team, seven of whom are active during play and five of whom are substitutes.

.The match is played in the best-of-three-set format with a rally score and 25 points in each set.

.The players must use both hands to catch the ball and only one hand to return it.

.The player's shoulder/shoulder line should be used as the release point for the ball during serving.

.No net contact should be made by the serving ball.

.In order to receive the service ball, no double touches are permitted.

.When the whistle blows, the players have five seconds to serve the ball.

.Although they shouldn't cross the end line, players should serve the ball from the service zone.
.Players are not permitted to move the ball from right to left or left to right since doing so results in penalty points.

.There are two timeouts of 30 seconds each over the whole contest.

.The players must don the right attire, which consists of shorts and a jersey with numbers printed on the front and back.

.Only the numbers 1 through 12 may be used.

.The ball cannot be caught by two players at once otherwise the team will lose points.

.Throwball is played on a court that measures 12.2 metres by 18.3 metres overall, with neutral boxes of 1 metres on either side of the whole court.

.At a height of 2.2 metres, the net is positioned in the middle of the court.
